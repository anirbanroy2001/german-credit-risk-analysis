Deploy Two-Gate Underwriting Model
Auto-approve applicants with prob < 0.30. Route prob 0.30–0.60 to manual underwriter review with a pre-filled risk summary. Decline or require collateral for prob > 0.60. At threshold 0.30, this catches 95.7% of all bad loans while wrongly declining only 6 good applicants per 200 assessed.
Highest Impact
2
Implement Purpose-Based Pricing
Vacation and education loans carry 44–67% bad rates. Require a 25–50% deposit or co-signer for these purposes. Furniture and radio/TV loans (15–18% bad rate) can be approved at standard terms with no additional requirements. Risk-based pricing is standard practice at all tier-1 lending institutions.
High Impact
3
Mandate Account Verification at Origination
Require at least one active savings or checking account with 3+ months history as a minimum origination criterion. Applicants with no savings AND no checking account carry a 50%+ bad rate — the double vulnerability flag is the strongest underwriting signal in the dataset and can be applied as a rule without any model.
High Impact
4
Set Monthly Burden Threshold Rule
Cap the credit-to-duration ratio at DM 250/month for unsecured loans. Bad risk applicants average DM 200/month burden versus DM 133/month for good risk applicants. This operationalises the model's most important engineered feature as a simple, auditable underwriting rule requiring no ML infrastructure to implement.
